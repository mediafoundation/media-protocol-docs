### Introduction

Media Network protocol is a peer-to-peer [^1] system crafted to enable the creation of decentralized marketplaces on the blockchain. This protocol is built as a set of persistent, non-upgradable smart contracts; developed to prioritize censorship resistance, security, self-custody, and function devoid of any intermediaries who may impose selective restrictions.

Multiple versions of Media Network exist. Each is open source with distinctive attributes tailored to various marketplace needs. Every iteration of Media Network, once initialized, will operate continuously with absolute uptime, contingent upon the enduring existence of the underlying blockchain.

### How does Media Network compare to typical platforms?
To fathom the distinctive nature of Media Network in comparison to regular marketplaces, it's imperative to delve into two arenas: the shift from centralized marketplace platforms to decentralized ones, and how permissionless systems differ from their permissioned counterparts.

### Centralized Marketplaces vs Decentralized Ones
Most prevalent marketplaces operate on centralized platforms, where the operator or entity oversees transactions, trust protocols, and manages the overall user experience. Anyone familiar with online shopping platforms will recognize this centralized model.

Media Network Protocol diverges from this model by offering a decentralized architecture. Instead of a single entity overseeing transactions, the protocol allows for P2P interactions, enabling anyone to establish and control their own marketplace.

At its core, this decentralized approach empowers users to directly interact, transact, and create without intermediary interference. This transformative methodology is attracting significant attention for its potential to democratize e-commerce and marketplace operations.

### Permissionless Systems
Another deviation from conventional platforms is Media Network's permissionless and immutable nature. These design choices are drawn from blockchain's foundational principles, and our unwavering commitment to the ethos of unrestricted access and unalterable operations as crucial for a future where anyone globally can establish marketplaces without facing discrimination or counter-party risks.

A permissionless architecture ensures that the protocol is fully accessible to the public without any barriers or selective restrictions. Anyone can create, manage, or participate in marketplaces at their discretion. This is a marked departure from traditional platforms that might limit access based on various criteria.

Moreover, the protocol is immutable, meaning it is steadfast and non-upgradable. No individual or entity can halt, reverse, or modify the protocol's inherent functionalities. While there may be guiding principles for the operation of certain marketplaces, the core protocol remains unaffected, guaranteeing trust and reliability for all its users.

[^1]: Blockchain protocols are sometimes referred to as peer-to-contract systems as well. These are similar to a peer-to-peer systems, but with immutable, persistent programs known as smart contracts taking the place of a peer.

:::note
### Disclaimer
Media Network is a decentralized, peer-to-peer system built on the blockchain, which facilitates direct interactions in our marketplace. We utilize smart contracts to ensure clarity and fairness in all operations. However, users are responsible for their actions, and Media Network does not oversee or regulate individual activities. We urge all participants to be diligent in protecting their personal and sensitive information. While the Media Foundation played a role in developing Media Network, we don't have centralized control or authority over it. Once the protocol is live, it operates independently, and neither its creators, the foundation, nor any third party can alter or remove it. Always exercise caution and be informed about your interactions on the platform.
:::
